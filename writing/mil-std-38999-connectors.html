An Introduction to MIL-Spec Connectors - D38999 Series III

Introduction to MIL-Spec Connectors

A MIL-Spec connector is designed to prevent air infiltration, which can bring in moisture leading to corrosion. Additionally, these connectors offer high magnetic permeability to minimize signal interference in close proximity to AC signal cables. To achieve these properties, MIL-Spec connectors are primarily made from materials such as aluminium instead of steel or iron.

Assembly instructions for D38999 Series III

Correct assembly of these connectors requires tools like the DMC Crimping tool, Positioner, and Extraction tool. The assembly process involves stripping the wire to the required length, crimping the contact, and inserting the contact into the connector. Proper care must be taken during each of these steps to ensure a reliable connection.

MIL-Spec "Equivalent" Connectors

In addition to MIL-Spec connectors, some manufacturers produce "equivalent" connectors that mimic the form factors and ruggedness of the MIL-Spec designs. These are called "equivalent" because they are not made exactly to MIL-Spec standards but are similar in many ways. Major manufacturers like TE Connectivity, Cinch, and Glenair produce these equivalents in their respective product families.
